Affordable flight computer and long-range radio system

May 2025

After self-studying John D. Anderson’s Introduction to Flight and taking the course Introduction to Aerospace Engineering (AE1110x) at TU Delft in 2024, I was inspired to practice applying the concepts covered. After my first attempt failed (I think I must've dropped a negative somewhere in the theoretical flight performance parameters), I tackled the project again a few months later. This proved to be a very valuable experience in both unmanned aircraft design/fabrication and avionics engineering. Although the aerodynamic equations involved were intense and filled countless papers from wall to wall, I also liked to think of this as a puzzle.

Eq. 7.37 from Anderson is a brilliant example of one of the components of the puzzle-like nature of this project very well, in which I had to solve, derive, or approximate quantities one by one until a satisfactory solution was reached, (quite literally) checking them off as I went. The formula gives an exact numerical value for the moment about the center of gravity, which is then plugged in to solve for the optimal elevator trim angle such that \(C_{M, cg} = M_{cg} = 0\). The equation is as follows:
\(C_{M,\text{cg}} = C_{M,\text{ac,wb}} + C_{L,wb}\,(h - h_{ac}) + FINISH LATEX \)
It's genuinely a beautiful formula, and many quantities here are actually simple. OK, I'm done getting excited and geeking out over formulas. Let's move on.

- Created a custom open-source, low-budget flight computer, joystick input interface, and radio communication protocol.

- Designed and fabricated a model unmanned aerial vehicle with a tuned airfoil, using a variety of materials and tools.
